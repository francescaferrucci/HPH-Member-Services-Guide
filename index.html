‚úÖ Check-In Protocol

Basic Formula

Appt type, time: reason for visit - presenting complaint
Example: MA, 10:00: Recheck liver values

Key Rules:

  • Reason for visit is what category was selected while booking
  • Time is always entered in military format
  • Pet's labeled time should be appointment time, NOT check-in time

Check-In Types

Drop-Offs (AM/PM)

Format: D/O AM/PM: reason - presenting complaint
Example: D/O AM: wellness - acupuncture
Action: Admit into 'Ready for Exam'

Walk-Ins

Format: W/I [time]: reason - presenting complaint
Example: W/I 14:05: injury - torn toenail
Action: Admit into 'Ready for Exam', highlight (Pet Parent Waiting)

Medical Appointments

Format: Medical [time]: reason - presenting complaint
Example: Medical 09:00: wellness - annual PE/Vx/TNT
Action: Leave in outpatient, PAGE BACK to notify
Late Arrivals: If 10+ minutes late, ask nurse if can still be seen. If not, nurse will advise drop-off or walk-in.

ü©∫ Diagnostic Testing "ABD"

A - ACTH Stimulation Test

  • Fasting not required
  • Pre draw + ONE hour post draw
  • Results typically same day

B - Bile Acids

  • MUST fast 12 hours prior
  • Pre draw + TWO hour post draw
  • Samples sent to reference lab
  • We call when results return

D - Low-Dose Dexamethasone Suppression Test

  • Fasting not required
  • Pre draw + FOUR hour + EIGHT hour post
  • Results typically same day
LATEST DROP-OFF FOR LDDST IS 10 AM! (Earlier is better)

‚ö†Ô∏è Triage - Immediate Attention Needed

Pets with these symptoms need URGENT CARE - notify medical immediately

  • Vehicle trauma within last 12-24 hours
  • Labored/rapid/difficulty breathing
  • Inability to urinate
  • Active bleeding
  • Sudden weakness (falling over/unable to stand)
  • Inability to use rear legs
  • Abdominal enlargement
  • Not eating for over 24 hours (NOTHING AT ALL!)
  • Pets with chronic illnesses that have relapsed
  • Dogs under 4 months with diarrhea, vomiting, no appetite
  • Male cats straining/unable to urinate

Triage Question Guides

Vomiting/Diarrhea Questions:

  • When was pet last 100% normal?
  • When did symptoms start?
  • Frequency of vomiting/diarrhea?
  • Last time pet ate/drank?
  • Description of vomit/stool?
  • Possible ingestion of foreign material?
  • Other pets in house with symptoms?
  • Current medications/treatments?
  • Recent contact with other pets?
